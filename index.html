Academic Interests

  • Computational Statistics

    How do we learn from data, and how do we know what we learned makes sense? Can we design algorithms robust to real world examples, where data is not perfect? How do we make good decisions when we don't have all the information?

  • Modeling behavior

    I am interested in using statistical methods to model individual decision makers. This ranges from trying to understand how people play games differently, to how people vote in elections, to how the range of individual preferences affect large-scale behavior.

  • Strategy Games

    Games provide closed environments for understanding the landscape of individual decision making. The study of games goes beyond learning how to play optimally; we also want to know how people actually play, and how different environments affect strategy and behavior.
